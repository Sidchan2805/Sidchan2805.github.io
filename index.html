Hi, I am Koustubh!

Graduate student at University of Maryland

I am pursuing a Master of Engineering degree in Robotics at the University of Maryland, where I apply the principles and technologies that drive the field. My interest in robotics stems from my Bachelor of Technology degree in Electrical Engineering from the Institute of Engineering and Technology, Lucknow. I have hands-on experience in developing and programming mobile robots and UAVs using Robot Operating System (ROS), which I gained from several academic and professional projects. I also have valuable skills in Salesforce Commerce Cloud (SFCC), which I used to monitor and maintain the storefront performance for a leading e-commerce client as an Assistant System Engineer at Tata Consultancy Services for 1.5 years. I enjoy attending conferences and hackathons to stay updated on the latest developments and trends in robotics. I am excited to continue learning and growing in this field and to contribute to its advancement.

Projects

Turtlebot Perception Challenge

ROS2 based perception package for TurtleBot3 enabling it to follow a paper trail while doing dynamic obstacle and stop sign detection. The project uses ROS2, YOLO and OpenCV

Survey and Rescue drone

A mini quadcopter programmed to effectivelty plan and navigate waypoints based on priority. The drone uses ROS, OpenCV and a path planner with PID controller to easily navigate trough waypoints.

  • More

Genetic Algorithm Based Path Planner

This project explores path planning using Genetic Algorithms (GA) to optimize navigation in complex environments. By leveraging evolutionary principles such as selection, crossover, and mutation, the algorithm finds efficient paths while avoiding obstacles.

  • More

Test Driven development and CI/CD for robotics using ROS2

A ROS2 simulation based project demostrating important software development practices such as Test Driven Development, Continuous Integration and Continuous Deployment, etc for ROS2 based projects

  • More

Predicting Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) using Eye Tracking Data and Deep Learning

This project uses deep learning to predict Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) from eye-tracking data. We apply CNNs, LSTMs, and Transformers to analyze the data and improve classification accuracy, aggregating multiple eye-tracking images per individual with sequence modeling to enhance diagnostic reliability.
